# End-to-end showcase: from a corrected Higgs measure to a GW spectrum

This example is a **complete pipeline** that takes a concrete finite-temperature effective potential,
builds the full **thermal history** (phases + transitions), finds the **nucleation temperature**,
solves the **bounce** at $T_n$, and finally computes the **gravitational-wave (GW) spectrum**
generated by the first-order phase transition.

The goal is not just to “run a code”, but to **see every physical step** in a sequence of
clean figures (A…O), each one validating a specific piece of the story.

The full script to this example can be found and run at: 

```bash
python docs/examples/Paper_Glauber/example_paper_GWs.py
````

---

## Motivation: why this potential produces a first-order transition

The potential used here is the one discussed in the paper *[“Cosmological phase transitions from the functional measure”](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2502.09593)*.
The basic idea is that a **modified functional measure** changes the structure of the effective potential.
This is not a random model-building trick: it is a correction to how the functional integral is defined and regulated,
and it modifies the effective potential by an additional term with a characteristic branch point.

Concretely, the total effective potential is written schematically as
$V(\phi,T)=V_0(\phi)+\Delta V_T(\phi,T)+\Delta V_{\rm daisy}(\phi,T)$,
where

- $V_0(\phi)$ contains the tree-level Higgs potential plus one-loop (Coleman–Weinberg–like) corrections
  and the **functional-measure** contribution;
- $\Delta V_T$ is the finite-temperature contribution computed from thermal integrals;
- $\Delta V_{\rm daisy}$ is an (optional) resummation term that captures leading plasma effects.

The measure deformation introduces the combination
$t(\phi)=C\,\phi^2/\Lambda^2$,
with a special point at
$\phi_\star=\Lambda/\sqrt{C}$,
where the logarithm in the measure term becomes singular (and the paper discusses how to interpret the domain).

The consequence is that the potential can develop a **barrier** between $\phi=0$ and $\phi\sim v$,
making the electroweak-like transition **first order**.

## What this script actually does (the pipeline)

This example glues together three layers of the project:

1. **Generic finite-$T$ potential interface (`generic_potential`)**
   - wraps a user-supplied $V(\phi,T)$ into the canonical $(X,T)$ interface expected by `transitionFinder`;
   - builds numerical derivatives:
     $$
     \nabla_X V,\qquad \nabla_X^2 V,\qquad \frac{\partial V}{\partial T},\qquad
     \frac{\partial}{\partial T}\left(\nabla_X V\right).
     $$

2. **Thermal history (`transitionFinder`)**
   - traces temperature-dependent minima (phases),
   - finds critical temperatures $T_c$,
   - searches for nucleation temperatures $T_n$ using a nucleation criterion.

3. **Bounce + gravitational waves (`SingleFieldInstanton` + `gravitational_Waves`)**
   - solves the bounce at $T=T_n$,
   - extracts $S_3(T_n)$ and related diagnostics,
   - computes $(\alpha,\beta/H_\*,R_\*H_\*)$ and the GW spectrum $h^2\Omega_{\rm GW}(f)$.

## Conventions and key formulas used in the example

### Nucleation condition

By default, nucleation is taken to occur when
$$
\frac{S_3(T)}{T}\approx 140,
$$
implemented through a criterion function of the form
$$
\text{nuclCriterion}(S,T)=\frac{S}{T}-140.
$$

### Thermal scales

- $T_c$: the **critical** temperature where the two phases are degenerate:
  $$
  \Delta V(T_c)=V_{\rm high}(T_c)-V_{\rm low}(T_c)=0.
  $$
- $T_n$: the **nucleation** temperature where tunneling becomes efficient.
- Spinodals: temperatures where the minimum loses stability, detected by the smallest Hessian eigenvalue
  crossing zero:
  $$
  m^2(T)=\min{\rm eig}\left[\nabla^2 V(\phi_{\min}(T),T)\right]\approx 0.
  $$

### GW variables

The GW module is fed by the transition summary and returns (at $T_*\approx T_n$):
- $\alpha(T_*)$: transition strength (latent heat relative to radiation density),
- $\beta/H_*$: inverse duration of the transition in Hubble units,
- $R_*H_*$: characteristic bubble scale in Hubble units,
- peak frequencies for the different GW sources, and the full spectrum
  $$
  h^2\Omega_{\rm GW}(f)=h^2\Omega_{\rm sw}(f)+h^2\Omega_{\rm turb}(f)+h^2\Omega_{\rm coll}(f).
  $$

### Example C — Key thermal scales: $T_n$, $T_c$, and spinodals

**Code:** `example_C_transition_summary(...)`  
**Outputs:** `figC_<tag>.png`

This is the first “real” finite-$T$ physics step.

It builds phases with `transitionFinder`, identifies the main first-order transition,
and extracts:

- $T_n$ (nucleation),
- $T_c$ (degeneracy),
- spinodals (loss of stability) of both phases via $m^2(T)\approx 0$.

**Purpose:**
- certify that the thermal history is consistent,
- show supercooling: typically $T_n<T_c$,
- bracket nucleation between instability limits whenever possible.

**What to look for physically:**
- is $T_n$ finite and inside a sensible interval,
- are there clear spinodals,
- does the ordering $T_{\rm sp}^{\rm high} \gtrsim T_c \gtrsim T_n \gtrsim T_{\rm sp}^{\rm low}$ make sense for this model.

### Example M — ODE term decomposition along the profile

**Code:** `example_M_ode_terms(...)`  
**Outputs:** `figM_<tag>.png`

The bounce equation (in the one-field O(3) case) can be written schematically as
$$
\phi''(r)+\frac{\alpha}{r}\phi'(r)=\frac{dV}{d\phi}.
$$

This example plots, as functions of $r$:

- $\phi''(r)$ (acceleration),
- $(\alpha/r)\phi'(r)$ (friction),
- $V'(\phi(r))$ (force),

and overlays $V(\phi(r))$ on a secondary axis.

**Purpose:**
- show explicitly where friction dominates,
- show where the force term drives the field motion,
- make the ODE mechanics transparent.


> ![figM](Paper_Glauber/results_C_3.65/figM_paper.png)
